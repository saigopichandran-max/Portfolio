01 — Scenario & Prospect Profile

Client Context & Presenting Challenge

A senior sales director at Goldman Sachs Asset Management’s Third‑Party Wealth (TPW) Switzerland desk was scheduled for an introductory meeting with a Swiss UHNW prospect: a multi‑generational family office in Geneva with CHF 80m in investable assets. The family’s wealth was built through a successful industrial business, now held in a holding structure alongside liquid investments.

The current portfolio was concentrated 65% in Swiss equities (SMI constituents, primarily Nestlé, Novartis, Roche, UBS) and 35% in cash/money market funds. The family had no formal alternatives exposure and minimal international diversification. They expressed interest in “diversifying internationally with some alternatives exposure” but were unsure about liquidity trade‑offs, fee levels, and manager selection.

The sales director requested a 2‑page pre‑meeting briefing note that would: (1) summarise the prospect’s current risk/return profile, (2) frame the opportunity for international diversification and alternatives, (3) suggest 2–3 GSAM products with compelling rationale, (4) anticipate likely objections, and (5) compare GSAM’s offering against UBS AM and Pictet — the two managers the family would most likely be familiar with through their Swiss private bank.

Key Constraint
The family’s cash allocation was held partly as dry powder for a potential real estate transaction; therefore any proposed alternatives allocation would need to be sized and structured to preserve liquidity for that near‑term need (estimated CHF 10–15m). The briefing had to offer a clear “execution roadmap” with phased implementation.

02 — Research & Analysis

Key Analytical Workstreams for the Briefing Note

Home‑Country Bias & Risk Quantification

Using Bloomberg, I analysed the Swiss Market Index’s sector concentration (pharmaceuticals ~35%, financials ~20%) and its correlation to global equity markets (0.68 with MSCI World). The prospect’s 65% allocation to Swiss equities exposed the portfolio to single‑country concentration risk without a compensating illiquidity premium.

  • SMI’s 3‑year volatility: 14.2% vs MSCI World 16.5% — lower volatility but higher drawdown risk during CHF strength episodes.
  • Cash drag: 35% in money market funds yielding 1.25% vs Swiss CPI 1.8% → negative real return of −0.55% on that sleeve.
Alternatives Opportunity Sizing

I modelled a “core‑satellite” reallocation that shifts CHF 16m (20% of the portfolio) from cash and a portion of Swiss equities into three GSAM strategies:

  • Private credit (Direct Lending) – senior secured, floating rate, 3‑year lock‑up, estimated net yield 7.5–8.5%.
  • Infrastructure Partners (Article 8) – 8–10% target net return, 0.18 correlation to equities.
  • Global Multi‑Asset Income – daily liquidity, 4–6% distribution yield, Sharpe 1.21.

03 — Recommendation (Briefing Note)

Tailored Product Narrative & Implementation Roadmap

Three‑Pillar Proposal

Pillar 1: Reduce home‑country bias — Rotate 15% (CHF 12m) from Swiss equities into the GSAM Global Multi‑Asset Income Fund, immediately improving diversification and adding a 4‑6% income stream.

Pillar 2: Add private credit (yield anchor) — Allocate CHF 8m (10%) to GSAM Direct Lending Fund. Floating‑rate structure provides protection against residual rate hikes and a 3‑year lock‑up aligns with the family’s liquidity buffer.

Pillar 3: Infrastructure for inflation & diversification — CHF 8m (10%) to GSAM Infrastructure Partners, adding an uncorrelated, CPI‑linked asset class that also meets ESG criteria (Article 8).

Together these changes target a portfolio blended return of CPI + 3.6% (above the client’s threshold), while keeping ≥ 40% of assets in highly liquid instruments.

Phased Implementation Plan (0–6 months)
  • ✓ Month 1: Move CHF 8m from cash into Direct Lending (immediate yield pickup).
  • ✓ Month 2: Rotate CHF 12m from Swiss equities to Multi‑Asset Income (daily liquidity, diversification).
  • ✓ Month 3–4: Commit CHF 8m to Infrastructure Partners (Q2 vintage close).
  • ✓ Month 6: Review liquidity position: CHF 32m remains in cash + liquid funds, well above the CHF 10‑15m real estate buffer.

05 — Outcome & Reflection

Impact & Key Learnings

🎯
Three Tailored Conversation Starters
The briefing note gave the sales director three specific openers: (1) “Your cash yield is eroding purchasing power — let’s look at a conservative private credit solution.” (2) “Swiss equities are high quality but concentrated — how do you feel about adding global multi‑asset income?” (3) “Infrastructure could give you inflation protection that gilts can’t.”
⚡
Objection‑Handling Framework
Pre‑researched answers for the top five objections (liquidity, fees, manager track record, ESG, timing) were embedded in the note, enabling the director to respond fluidly without checking notes. This reduced preparation time for the director by an estimated 70%.
📈
Competitive Positioning Confidence
The competitive matrix highlighted GSAM’s yield advantage in private credit (80‑150bps over UBS AM) and stronger infrastructure platform. The director used this to differentiate during the meeting — a critical element in winning against incumbent providers.
