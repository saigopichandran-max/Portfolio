01 · Overview

What This Report Is — and Why It Was Built

This project was conceived as a direct response to a recurring challenge in wealth management sales: senior sales professionals spend significant time preparing product knowledge from scratch before client meetings. This report was designed to serve as a comprehensive, standing reference — the kind a Sales Support Analyst would produce and maintain in a Third-Party Wealth team.

Goldman Sachs Asset Management manages over $2.5 trillion in AUM across some of the most sophisticated multi-asset strategies in the industry. The breadth of the product shelf — spanning daily-liquidity public market funds through to decade-long lock-up private equity vehicles — creates a real knowledge management challenge for anyone supporting external distribution teams.

This 15-page report solves that problem. It provides a structured, analyst-written overview of GSAM's key strategies across six asset classes, including risk-adjusted performance analysis, fee decomposition, peer benchmarking, and a client segmentation layer mapping products to specific wealth client profiles.

Every data point in the report was sourced, cross-referenced, and validated using Bloomberg Terminal, GSAM's published fund factsheets, and public regulatory filings — reflecting the data integrity disciplines applied in a professional investment analyst role.

Finding 01
Multi-Asset Income offers best risk-adjusted return for Swiss mandates
Sharpe ratio of 1.21 — highest across all liquid strategies — and max drawdown of only −8.6%. Aligns with income needs of retired UHNW clients.
Finding 02
Private credit offers compelling yield for clients accepting lock-ups
Gross yield SOFR+550–650bps, net ~7.8% after stress — 460bps pickup over Swiss IG corporates. Strong case for 5–10% allocation.
Finding 03
GSAM's alternatives fees are competitive, public equity fees are premium-priced
Public equity fees (50–90bps) sit in second quartile vs. peers; alternatives fees (1.25–1.5% / 15–20%) are at or below market.
Finding 04
Infrastructure's low correlation is a unique portfolio tool
Correlation to MSCI ACWI of 0.18. Adding 10% reduces portfolio volatility by 0.8pp and improves Sharpe from 0.72 to 0.81.
Finding 05
Duration risk is the defining fixed income factor for Swiss clients
Short Duration Bond Fund's −2.1% drawdown in 2022 vs. −16.2% for benchmark — recommended as core fixed income building block.
Finding 06
EM Equity is suitable only for a selective client subset
Sharpe 0.46, max drawdown −32.7% — not appropriate for capital preservation or risk-averse Swiss clients.
Finding 07
GSAM's PE programme justifies illiquidity premium on PME basis
PME analysis: CHF 5m commitment outperformed GSAM Global Equity Fund by 4.2% net IRR over 10-year fund life.
Finding 08
ESG alignment is a commercial priority for Swiss market
Article 8 classification for Infrastructure Fund and net-zero commitment positions GSAM well for Swiss RFP processes requiring ESG documentation.
