01 · Context & Objective

Why This Pitch Deck Was Built

In wealth management, the ability to prepare a compelling, structured pitch for a client meeting is one of the most commercially valuable skills an analyst can demonstrate. Senior sales professionals rely on their support teams to synthesise market data, product analysis, and client-specific considerations into a coherent narrative — one that is both technically rigorous and emotionally persuasive.

This project simulates that exact process. The brief: construct a 10-slide investment pitch for a hypothetical Swiss UHNW family — the Kellers of Geneva, with CHF 50m in investable assets — who have expressed interest in diversifying beyond their current domestic equity and cash holdings. Their specific instruction: they would like to allocate 20% (CHF 10m) into alternatives, but they have never done so before and want a clear, evidence-based rationale before committing.

The pitch was built using Bloomberg Terminal data, GSAM published fund materials, and publicly available Swiss market data — and was structured to meet the presentation standards expected by a TPW (Third-Party Wealth) sales team at a global asset manager. It focuses on three GSAM alternative strategies: Private Credit (Direct Lending), Infrastructure Partners, and Vintage Private Equity.

The finished deck was reviewed by a senior finance professional, who assessed the investment rationale, visual design, risk framing, and client communication quality as "presentation-ready" — meaning it could be used in an actual client meeting with minimal modification.

Every data point, chart, and recommendation in the deck is grounded in real market data. The risk comparisons use Bloomberg-sourced volatility and return figures. The fee structures reflect actual GSAM fund terms. The regulatory references are accurate to Swiss FinSA requirements as at 2024.

Agenda — Structuring the Conversation

A well-designed agenda slide does two things: it signals that the presenter is organised, and it gives the client a mental map of the meeting. For a UHNW client who values their time above almost anything else, this is a critical trust signal.

  • Five-point agenda — concise, respecting the 45-minute meeting slot
  • Client-centric framing: "Your Portfolio Today" not "Market Overview" — every section title references the client's situation, not the presenter's material
  • Includes a "Next Steps" section — signals that this is a decision-oriented meeting, not an information session
  • No time allocation shown — flexibility to spend more time where client interest is highest

05 · Methodology

How the Pitch Was Built — Five-Phase Process

1

Client Research & Profile Construction

The first phase involved constructing a detailed and realistic client profile — the foundation on which the entire pitch rests. The Keller family profile was designed to reflect genuine Swiss UHNW characteristics: a CHF 50m portfolio dominated by domestic equities and cash, a long investment horizon driven by intergenerational wealth transfer goals, strong ESG preferences, and a conservative-to-balanced risk appetite. The existing portfolio's current estimated return (CPI + 0.8%) was modelled using Bloomberg data on the Swiss Market Index, Swiss government bond yields, and SNB cash rates — establishing the return gap that the pitch needs to close.

2

Product Selection & Deep Analysis

Three GSAM alternative strategies were selected from the full product universe (analysed in Project 01 — the GSAM Coverage Report) based on their fit with the Keller profile: Direct Lending (yield solution), Infrastructure (inflation protection + diversification), and Vintage PE (long-term growth). For each product, a detailed analysis was conducted: yield decomposition, fee modelling, stress testing, ESG verification, and Swiss FinSA suitability assessment. Bloomberg data was used to pull historical volatility, correlation, and return series where available, supplemented by GSAM published fund materials for private market strategies.

GSAM Fund FactsheetsBloomberg Fund AnalyticsFee Modelling (Excel)Stress Testing
3

Portfolio Construction & Return Modelling

The three products were sized and combined into a CHF 10m alternatives sleeve. Allocation sizing was guided by three principles: diversification (no single alternative exceeding 8% of total portfolio), liquidity tiering (shorter lock-up products allocated first), and return contribution (each product must contribute meaningfully to the CPI + 3% target). The blended portfolio return was modelled in Excel using weighted average expected returns for each asset class. Portfolio-level volatility was estimated using a correlation matrix (sourced from Bloomberg for liquid assets, and from published academic research on illiquid alternatives correlation). The resulting Sharpe ratio improvement (0.58 → 0.74) was calculated and validated.

Competitive Intelligence & Objection Preparation

Before finalising the deck, a competitive benchmarking exercise was conducted — comparing GSAM's three recommended products against the closest alternatives from UBS AM and Pictet (the two managers most likely to be on the Keller family's radar through their Swiss private bank). The comparison covered yield, fee terms, AUM platform size, ESG credentials, and minimum commitment levels. In parallel, five likely client objections were identified (drawn from common UHNW objections to alternatives) and prepared responses were written — these appear in the deck's appendix as a client FAQ.

06 · Objection Handling

Five Likely Client Objections — Prepared Responses

A pitch is only as good as the conversation it enables. For every potential client pushback, the deck's appendix includes a pre-prepared response — grounded in data, respectful of the client's concern, and always redirecting to the Kellers' specific goals. These were researched using common objections raised by Swiss UHNW families to alternatives exposure.

Objection 01 — Liquidity
"We don't want to lock up money for 10 years. What if we need it?"
The three recommended products have differentiated liquidity profiles. Direct Lending offers quarterly redemptions after year 3 — the fastest liquidity in the alternatives sleeve. Infrastructure distributions begin in year 2–3. Private Equity (only CHF 2m / 4% of portfolio) is the longest lock-up, but represents a small enough portion that it does not impair overall portfolio liquidity. The remaining 80% of the portfolio (CHF 40m) remains in fully liquid instruments — far more than adequate for any foreseeable liquidity event.
Objection 02 — Risk
"Alternatives sound risky. We don't want to lose capital."
Of the three recommended strategies, Direct Lending holds senior secured 1st lien positions — it is senior to equity and second lien debt in any borrower's capital structure, meaning it is recovered first in a default. Even at a 10% default rate with 40% recovery, the net yield of ~7.8% remains positive. Infrastructure revenues are mostly contracted and government-backed — among the most stable cash-flow profiles in any asset class. Capital loss at the portfolio level would require defaults far exceeding historical records.
Objection 03 — Fees
"The fees look much higher than our current funds."
Alternatives fees are higher in absolute terms — but the comparison that matters is net-of-fee return. A Swiss passive equity fund at 0.15% fees delivers approximately 7% gross — 6.85% net. GSAM's Direct Lending at 1.25% + 17.5% delivers approximately 10.5% gross — c.7.6–8.0% net after fees and estimated credit losses. The net return advantage of 90–115bps per annum on the alternatives allocation, compounded over 10 years, is meaningful on a CHF 4m position.
Objection 04 — Complexity
"We don't understand how these structures work. It seems very complicated."
This is a completely valid concern — and precisely why we have produced this document rather than a fund factsheet. The pitch deck is designed to give the Keller family full visibility into what they would own, how it generates returns, and what risks they are taking. Slide 6 shows exactly how the Direct Lending Fund lends money and gets repaid. Slide 7 shows specifically what infrastructure assets are owned and how they generate contracted revenues. We are also proposing to schedule a 30-minute Q&A call with the relevant GSAM portfolio manager before any commitment is signed.
Objection 05 — ESG / Values
"How do we know these investments align with our family's values?"
All three recommended products carry SFDR Article 8 classification, meaning they promote specific environmental or social characteristics and must report on them annually. The Infrastructure fund focuses explicitly on energy transition (renewable energy, EV charging infrastructure) and social infrastructure (schools, hospitals) — assets the Kellers' grandchildren will use. Full sustainability reports and SFDR Periodic Disclosure documents for each product are included in Appendix D, and can be reviewed by the family's independent adviser.
Objection 06 — Timing
"Is now the right time to invest in alternatives? Markets seem uncertain."
Market uncertainty is precisely when alternatives demonstrate their value. Private credit is floating-rate — it benefits directly from higher rates. Infrastructure's contracted, inflation-linked revenues are uncorrelated to equity market sentiment. Private equity's 3–4 year deployment period means capital is invested gradually, averaging into market conditions rather than committing at a single point. The 10–12 year investment horizon we are recommending is longer than any typical market cycle — timing matters far less over that horizon than the quality of the underlying assets.
07 · Key Findings

Six Analytical Conclusions From the Pitch Process

Finding 01
The Kellers' current portfolio is structurally incapable of meeting their return objective without alternatives
Modelling the current 55% SMI / 30% cash / 15% bonds allocation against realised returns, the portfolio delivers approximately CPI + 0.8% net — 220bps short of the stated CPI + 3% target. No combination of public market assets available in the Swiss market can close this gap without taking on significantly more equity risk. The only structurally sound path to CPI + 3% is through the illiquidity and complexity premiums available in alternatives.
Finding 02
A 20% alternatives allocation improves the portfolio Sharpe ratio from 0.58 to 0.74 — while reducing volatility
Counter-intuitively, adding "riskier" alternative assets to the Keller portfolio reduces overall portfolio volatility — from 9.2% to 8.1% — because alternatives' low correlation to Swiss equities provides genuine diversification. The Sharpe ratio improvement from 0.58 to 0.74 represents a 27% improvement in risk-adjusted returns. This is the strongest quantitative argument for the pitch: more return, less risk.
Finding 03
Private credit is the single most efficient return enhancement tool for the Keller profile
The Direct Lending Fund's stress-tested net yield of ~7.8% (at 10% default / 40% recovery) represents a 490bps pickup over Swiss IG corporates — the highest risk-adjusted yield differential of any strategy analysed. On a CHF 4m allocation, this generates approximately CHF 312,000 of additional annual income compared to the equivalent corporate bond allocation. The floating-rate structure also means this yield increases if the SNB keeps rates elevated — a convex benefit for the Kellers.
Finding 04
Infrastructure is the optimal inflation hedge — quantifiably superior to gold or inflation-linked bonds
Benchmarked against three traditional inflation hedges — gold, Swiss TIPS, and commodity funds — infrastructure's CPI-linked contracted revenues provide more reliable real-return protection with lower volatility. Gold has a 3Y volatility of 18.4% with no income; Swiss TIPS yield CPI + 0.1%; commodity funds show CPI correlation of 0.42. Infrastructure yields CPI + 4–5% with 0.18 equity correlation and 4–5% annual distributions. It is the superior inflation hedge on every quantitative dimension.
Finding 05
GSAM offers the best net-of-fee return among the three alternatives managers compared
Despite headline fees that are broadly comparable, GSAM's higher gross yield (SOFR + 550–650bps vs. UBS AM's SOFR + 450–520bps) means it delivers a superior net return even after accounting for the slightly higher performance fee. On a CHF 4m Direct Lending allocation over 5 years, GSAM's net advantage of ~80bps per annum compounds to approximately CHF 168,000 of additional cumulative return — a meaningful differential that justifies the GSAM recommendation.
Finding 06
A phased implementation approach reduces timing risk and manages the J-curve more effectively
Rather than committing CHF 10m immediately, the deck recommends a phased approach: Direct Lending (CHF 4m) committed in Q4 given quarterly redemptions reduce lock-up risk; Infrastructure (CHF 4m) in Q1 once regulatory documentation is complete; Private Equity (CHF 2m) in Q2 as part of GSAM's next vintage close. This approach ensures the Kellers are never simultaneously locked up across all three products, manages cash-flow timing, and gives the family time to review the Direct Lending investment before committing the full CHF 10m.
