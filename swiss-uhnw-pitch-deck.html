01 · Context & Objective

Why This Pitch Deck Was Built

In wealth management, the ability to prepare a compelling, structured pitch for a client meeting is one of the most commercially valuable skills an analyst can demonstrate. Senior sales professionals rely on their support teams to synthesise market data, product analysis, and client-specific considerations into a coherent narrative — both technically rigorous and emotionally persuasive.

This project simulates that exact process. The brief: construct a 10-slide investment pitch for a hypothetical Swiss UHNW family — the Kellers of Geneva, with CHF 50m in investable assets — who wish to allocate 20% (CHF 10m) into alternatives. The pitch was built using Bloomberg Terminal data, GSAM published fund materials, and publicly available Swiss market data — structured to meet the presentation standards of a TPW sales team.

The finished deck was reviewed by a senior finance professional and rated "presentation-ready" — meaning it could be used in an actual client meeting with minimal modification.

06 · Objection Handling

Five Likely Client Objections — Prepared Responses

Objection 01 — Liquidity
"We don't want to lock up money for 10 years. What if we need it?"
Direct Lending offers quarterly redemptions after year 3. Infrastructure distributions begin in year 2–3. Private Equity (only CHF 2m / 4%) is the longest lock-up but represents a small portion. Remaining 80% remains fully liquid.
Objection 02 — Risk
"Alternatives sound risky. We don't want to lose capital."
Direct Lending holds senior secured 1st lien positions — first in line in a default. Even at 10% default / 40% recovery, net yield remains positive. Infrastructure revenues are contracted and government-backed.
Objection 03 — Fees
"The fees look much higher than our current funds."
Alternatives fees are higher but net-of-fee return is what matters. Direct Lending net ~7.8% after costs and stress losses vs. Swiss IG corporates at 2.9% — a 490bps advantage.
Objection 04 — Complexity
"We don't understand how these structures work."
The pitch deck is designed to give full visibility — slide 6 shows exactly how lending works. We also offer a Q&A call with the GSAM portfolio manager before any commitment.
Objection 05 — ESG / Values
"How do we know these align with our family's values?"
All three products carry SFDR Article 8 classification. The Infrastructure fund focuses on energy transition and social infrastructure — assets the Kellers' grandchildren will use. Full sustainability reports included.
Objection 06 — Timing
"Is now the right time to invest?"
Private credit is floating-rate — benefits from higher rates. Infrastructure's contracted revenues are uncorrelated to equity sentiment. PE's deployment period averages into market conditions, reducing timing risk.
07 · Key Findings

Six Analytical Conclusions From the Pitch Process

Finding 01
Current portfolio structurally incapable of meeting return objective without alternatives
Current 55% SMI / 30% cash / 15% bonds delivers CPI + 0.8% net — 220bps short of CPI + 3% target. No public market combination can close this gap without excessive equity risk.
Finding 02
20% alternatives allocation improves Sharpe ratio from 0.58 to 0.74 while reducing volatility
Counter-intuitively, adding alternatives reduces total portfolio volatility (9.2% → 8.1%) due to low correlation. Sharpe improvement of 27% is the strongest quantitative argument.
Finding 03
Private credit is the most efficient return enhancement tool
Stress‑tested net yield of ~7.8% represents 490bps pickup over Swiss IG corporates. On CHF 4m, that's ~CHF 312k additional annual income.
Finding 04
Infrastructure is the optimal inflation hedge — superior to gold or TIPS
CPI-linked contracted revenues provide reliable real‑return protection with low volatility. Yields CPI + 4–5% with 0.18 equity correlation.
Finding 05
GSAM offers best net‑of‑fee return among compared managers
Higher gross yield (SOFR + 550–650bps) delivers ~80bps net advantage over UBS AM on a CHF 4m allocation over 5 years (~CHF 168k additional cumulative return).
Finding 06
Phased implementation reduces timing risk and manages J‑curve
Commit in stages: Direct Lending (Q4), Infrastructure (Q1), Private Equity (Q2). Avoids simultaneous lock‑up, manages cash‑flow timing, gives client review period.
