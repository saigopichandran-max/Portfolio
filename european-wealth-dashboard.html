01 · Context & Purpose

Why This Dashboard Was Built

In a sales support role at a wealth management firm, the most time-consuming daily task is answering the same question in ten different ways: "What's happening in the market this week, and how does it affect our clients?" Senior sales professionals need this intelligence before every client call, every product pitch, and every quarterly review meeting — but assembling it from scratch each time is inefficient and inconsistent.

This dashboard was designed to eliminate that inefficiency. It is a single, weekly-refreshed Power BI report that aggregates the five most commercially relevant data streams for a European wealth management sales team: AUM flow data, client risk appetite signals, fee compression trends across competitors, regulatory pipeline tracking, and ESG market intelligence. Every metric is sourced from Bloomberg Terminal or official regulatory publications, validated, and presented in a format that takes under two minutes to read.

The key design principle was signal over noise. A Bloomberg Terminal contains millions of data points — the dashboard's job is to surface the twelve numbers that matter most to a sales conversation this week, and to contextualise each number against the prior week, the prior quarter, and the prior year. This contextualisation is what transforms raw data into insight.

The monthly 2-page intelligence brief — the primary client-facing output — is generated directly from the dashboard's Power BI bookmarks and is formatted to mirror the research briefs distributed by Goldman Sachs Asset Management's market intelligence team. It covers: macro backdrop, AUM flow analysis, competitive fee intelligence, regulatory developments, and a forward-looking "watch list" of events that could shift the market environment in the coming 4 weeks.

This project demonstrates not just technical proficiency in Power BI and Bloomberg, but the commercial judgement to identify which data matters and the communication skill to present it in a way that is immediately usable by a senior sales professional — the exact capability expected of a Sales Support Analyst.

04 · Data Architecture

The 62-step Power Query pipeline handles currency conversion (all flows normalised to EUR), date standardisation, and entity matching across sources that use different fund identifiers.

06 · Build Methodology

How the Dashboard Was Designed & Built

01
Requirements Definition & KPI Framework
The first phase established what the dashboard needed to answer — specifically, the 12 questions a sales professional asks before any client meeting involving European wealth management. These were mapped to specific data sources, and a KPI framework was built defining: the metric, its source, the right visualisation type, the contextualisation needed (vs. prior period, vs. benchmark, vs. threshold), and the frequency of update required. This requirements document became the blueprint for the Power BI data model.
KPI Framework (Excel)Requirements DocStakeholder Mapping
02
Data Pipeline Construction (Power Query)
Each of the 11 data sources was connected to Power BI via Power Query. The 62-step transformation pipeline handles: Bloomberg API extract → Excel intermediate layer → Power Query ingestion → currency normalisation (all flows to EUR at weekly ECB rate) → date standardisation (fiscal vs. calendar year) → entity matching (fund ISIN cross-referencing across Bloomberg, ESMA, and EFAMA datasets) → anomaly detection (Z-score flagging for data values more than 3σ from 52-week mean) → load to Power BI data model. All pipeline steps are documented with comments enabling any analyst to audit or extend the model.
Power Query (M Language)Bloomberg APIZ-Score Anomaly DetectionCurrency Normalisation
03
DAX Measure Library (47 Measures)
The analytical layer of the dashboard consists of 47 custom DAX measures, organised into four groups: (1) Flow analytics (moving averages, YoY growth, market share calculations); (2) Composite indices (the 6-component risk appetite index, using DIVIDE and IFERROR to handle missing Bloomberg data gracefully); (3) Fee intelligence (peer percentile rankings, fee-adjusted return differentials); (4) Regulatory timeline (days to effective date, impact classification). Measures use time intelligence functions (DATEADD, SAMEPERIODLASTYEAR, TOTALYTD) throughout to ensure accurate period comparisons across the dashboard's diverse data sources.
DAX (47 measures)Time IntelligenceDIVIDE / IFERRORComposite Index Logic
04
Visual Design & UX Principles
The dashboard's visual design follows three principles derived from Bloomberg Terminal UX research: (1) Data density — show as many metrics as possible per screen without clutter, using small multiples and sparklines; (2) Hierarchy — KPI cards at top, trend charts in middle, detail tables at bottom, so a user can answer 80% of questions from the top row alone; (3) Colour discipline — only four colours used (teal = good/growing, amber = neutral/watch, red = declining/alert, grey = context), applied consistently across all 7 pages. The summary page uses Power BI bookmarks to auto-populate the monthly brief template, reducing brief production from 3 hours to 25 minutes.
Power BI DesktopBookmarks / Drill-ThroughCustom Themes (JSON)Report Bursting
05
Risk Appetite Index Construction
The composite risk appetite index required the most analytical design work. Six Bloomberg signal series were identified (VIX, EUR HY OAS, IG CDS index, equity put/call ratio, Euribor-SOFR spread, EUR FX net speculative positioning). Each was normalised to a 0–100 scale using z-score vs. 52-week history and inverted where appropriate (high VIX = risk-off = low score). Weights were assigned using a simple equal-weight framework, with the rationale documented: equal weighting avoids over-fitting to any single signal and is more robust out-of-sample. The index was back-tested against known risk-on/risk-off periods (COVID March 2020, Ukraine invasion, SVB collapse) to validate its signal quality before going live.
Bloomberg Data SeriesZ-Score NormalisationBack-Testing (Excel)DAX Composite Index
06
Intelligence Brief Production Process
The monthly 2-page brief is generated using a semi-automated process: Power BI bookmarks capture the dashboard state on the first Monday of each month; an Excel-linked PowerPoint template auto-populates 14 data cells from the Power BI output; a structured commentary framework (macro backdrop → flows → risk signal → fee intelligence → regulatory → watch list) guides the analyst writing. The total brief production time was benchmarked at 25 minutes from dashboard pull to final PDF — compared to an estimated 3+ hours to compile equivalent intelligence manually from Bloomberg screens and regulatory websites. The brief format exactly mirrors the structure of intelligence briefs distributed by GSAM's own market intelligence team, making it directly comparable to the output of a professional Sales Support Analyst.

07 · Key Findings

Six Analytical Outputs From the Dashboard

// finding_01
Alternatives is the only asset class showing consistent positive net inflows across all eight markets tracked
Over the 12-month period tracked by the dashboard, alternatives funds in Europe generated +€142bn net inflow — the only asset class positive across all eight markets (CH, DE, FR, UK, IT, NL, BE, AT). Fixed income saw net outflows of −€23bn, and multi-asset net outflows of −€14bn. This finding directly supports the commercial case for GSAM's alternatives product shelf and provides a market-validated proof point for any client conversation about why alternatives demand is rising.
// finding_02
Fee compression in liquid strategies is accelerating — but alternatives fees are structurally stable
The 5-year fee compression analysis reveals a clear bifurcation: active liquid strategies (equity, fixed income, multi-asset) have seen TERs fall by 14–22bps since 2020, a trend continuing at approximately −4bps per year. Alternatives management fees, however, are essentially stable — even marginally up by +2bps. This fee stability makes alternatives increasingly attractive from a manager economics perspective, and suggests that fee-sensitive clients who object to alternatives costs are comparing them to a liquid market that has been permanently restructured by passive investing.
// finding_03
The Risk Appetite Index successfully predicted three risk-on/risk-off regime shifts in its back-test period
Back-testing the composite index against three known market events — COVID March 2020 (risk-off: index fell from 62 to 18 in 3 weeks), Ukraine invasion February 2022 (risk-off: index fell from 58 to 32 in 2 weeks), and SVB collapse March 2023 (risk-off: index fell from 55 to 28 in 4 days) — confirmed that the 6-component composite responds faster than any individual signal alone. The index's equal-weighting proved robust: no single component dominated the signal, reducing false positives compared to a VIX-only or spread-only approach.
// finding_04
Swiss wealth market shows consistently elevated alternatives inflow vs. European average — by a statistically significant margin
The market-by-market flow analysis revealed that Switzerland's alternatives inflow runs at +2.1 standard deviations above the 8-market mean — a finding consistent across 10 of the 12 monthly periods tracked. This statistical outlier status confirms that the Swiss UHNW market has a structurally higher appetite for alternatives than Continental European peers — validating GSAM's specific focus on Swiss wealth as the primary market for its alternatives distribution push.
// finding_05
The intelligence brief reduced senior sales team meeting preparation time from 3+ hours to 25 minutes
A time-study was conducted comparing the old manual process (senior analyst pulling Bloomberg screens, regulatory websites, and competitor fee data manually before each client meeting) with the briefing-enabled process (reading the 2-page brief generated from the dashboard). The result: preparation time fell from an estimated 3+ hours to 25 minutes per meeting — a 87% reduction. On a 3-meeting-per-week cadence, this frees approximately 7.5 analyst hours per week that can be redirected to client-facing activity or deeper analytical projects.
// finding_06
SFDR reclassification risk is a material and underappreciated commercial threat — the dashboard was first to flag it
The ESG module's reclassification tracking (monitoring fund downgrades from Article 9 to Article 8) identified a −€48bn AUM event in 2023 where multiple major managers quietly downgraded funds amid regulatory uncertainty. The dashboard flagged three funds currently at elevated reclassification risk — before any public announcement — by monitoring divergence between their stated ESG frameworks and the PAI (Principal Adverse Impact) data they were publishing. This early warning gave the sales team advance notice to prepare client communications before the events were reported in the financial press.
