01 — Scenario

Client Background & Presenting Question

A hypothetical UK-based family office with £8 million in assets held a classic 60/40 portfolio — £4.8m in a diversified equity sleeve and £3.2m in investment-grade government and corporate bonds. The bond allocation carried a modified duration of approximately 7 years, leaving the portfolio meaningfully exposed to interest rate movements.

As the Bank of England and the Federal Reserve began their most aggressive hiking cycles in four decades — with the BoE raising rates from 0.25% (December 2021) to 5.25% by mid-2023 — the family office's investment adviser was asked: should we rebalance the fixed income sleeve, and if so, how?

The client had strong familiarity with traditional bond markets but limited exposure to floating-rate instruments and private credit alternatives. They sought a recommendation that balanced income generation, capital preservation, and portfolio liquidity.

Key Constraint
The client required at least 60% of the portfolio to remain liquid within a 90-day horizon, ruling out full rotation into illiquid private credit. A portion of the bond sleeve was also earmarked as collateral against a facility.
Macro Context
By Q2 2022, UK CPI had reached 9.1% — a 40-year high. Market consensus pointed to further rate rises. Bloomberg forward curves implied a peak BoE rate of 5.00–5.50% by mid-2023, implying continued duration pain for long-dated fixed income holders.

02 — Analysis

Duration Impact Modelling & Scenario Testing

Duration Stress Test — Rate Scenarios

Using Bloomberg duration analytics and a standard convexity-adjusted price approximation, I modelled the mark-to-market impact on the £3.2m fixed income sleeve under three rate-rise scenarios.

Formula applied: ΔP ≈ −D × ΔR × P

Key Methodology
  • Modified duration sourced from Bloomberg Terminal per security
  • Applied parallel shift assumption across the yield curve
  • Convexity adjustment applied for the 300bps scenario (non-linear effect)
  • Recovery in income yield modelled over 18-month horizon to assess breakeven
  • Liquidity constraints mapped against 90-day redemption windows per fund

Liquidity Constraint Mapping

Client requires £4.8m (60%) accessible within 90 days. The Gilt and corporate bond positions are daily liquidity but face MTM risk. Private credit solutions were assessed only for the portion not earmarked as collateral.
Collateral Restriction
Approximately £800,000 of the Gilt position is pledged as collateral. This portion was excluded from reallocation scenarios and modelled as a legacy hold with hedging overlay considerations.
Conclusion
Senior secured loans and short-duration credit satisfying the liquidity mandate while meaningfully reducing duration risk. Private credit capped at 10% of the overall portfolio given liquidity constraints.
03 — Recommendation

Phased Rebalancing Strategy

Core Recommendation

Implement a phased three-tranche rebalancing of the fixed income sleeve over 90 days, reducing portfolio-level modified duration from 7.0 to 3.5 years — cutting interest rate sensitivity by 50% — while maintaining full liquidity compliance and improving the income yield of the bond portfolio.

The reallocation pivots 30% of the bond sleeve into senior secured leveraged loans (floating rate, SOFR+400bps), replacing long-duration Gilt exposure at the margin. The remaining allocation shifts toward short-duration IG credit and inflation-linked instruments, retaining daily liquidity.

Rationale — Senior Secured Loans
Senior secured leveraged loans offered the most compelling risk-adjusted trade: floating-rate coupons (SOFR+400bps ≈ 9%+ by late 2022), near-zero duration, and senior secured seniority in the capital structure. Default rates at the time remained below 1.5%, and recovery rates historically average 70% at the senior secured level.

Implementation Timeline
Phase 1 — Days 0–30 (Immediate)

Reduce longest-duration Gilt exposure by rotating £960,000 (12% of AUM) from 10yr+ Gilts into a diversified senior secured loan fund. This achieves the fastest duration reduction and immediately positions the portfolio to benefit from rising SOFR/SONIA rates.

  • Sell £960k in 10yr+ Gilt ETF (IGLTs or similar)
  • Invest into a sterling-denominated senior secured loan fund
  • Duration impact: −1.8 years at portfolio level
Phase 2 — Days 30–60

Rotate a portion of IG corporate bonds into short-duration (1–3yr) investment-grade credit and inflation-linked bonds, improving the income profile and adding a hedge against persistent inflation.

  • Sell £800k of 5–7yr IG corporate bonds
  • Allocate £400k to 1–3yr IG credit; £400k to index-linked Gilts
  • Duration impact: additional −0.9 years at portfolio level
Phase 3 — Days 60–90 (Selective)

Optional tail allocation to private senior secured credit for the slice of the portfolio not subject to liquidity constraints (~£240k, 3% of AUM), capturing the significant illiquidity premium available in 2022–2023 private credit markets.

  • Subject to client confirmation and liquidity review
  • Target: private senior credit fund, 3–5 year lock-up
  • Expected yield: 9–11% with floating-rate structure
Equity Sleeve — Defensive Tilt

While the primary rebalancing focuses on the fixed income sleeve, I also recommended a defensive tilt within the equity allocation — increasing exposure to value/dividend sectors (utilities, healthcare) that historically outperform in rising rate environments, at the expense of long-duration growth equities.

  • Shift 10% of equity sleeve from growth/tech to value dividend
  • No change to total equity weight
  • Estimated yield uplift: +0.8% on the equity sleeve

05 — Outcome & Reflection

Results & Learning

📉
Duration Cut by 50%
The phased rebalancing reduces portfolio-level modified duration from 7.0 to 3.5 years, halving the sensitivity to further rate moves. Under the base case +200bps scenario, the MTM bond loss is reduced from £448,000 to an estimated £224,000.
📈
Income Yield Uplift
Rotating into senior secured loans (SOFR+400bps ≈ 9%) and short-duration IG credit (4.8–5.5%) materially improves the running yield of the fixed income sleeve — from approximately 2.8% (long Gilts in a low-rate environment) to an estimated 6.5–7.5% blended yield.
🔒
Liquidity Maintained
Over 90% of the rebalanced portfolio remains accessible within 90 days. The liquidity constraint is satisfied at each phase, and the 3% private credit tail allocation is structured as a clearly separate, discretionary decision for the client.
