01 · Overview

Why This Model Matters — and What It Proves

A Collateralised Loan Obligation is one of the most structurally complex instruments in credit markets. It takes a portfolio of leveraged loans — loans made to sub-investment-grade companies — pools them together, and issues multiple tranches of debt and equity against that pool, each with a different priority claim on the cash flows. The waterfall is the legal and economic engine that determines who gets paid, in what order, and under what conditions.

Understanding the waterfall mechanics is the defining competency for any analyst working in CLO management, structured credit, or leveraged finance. This model was built to demonstrate that understanding at a granular, functional level. The $200m notional portfolio of 80 leveraged loans is modelled with loan-level characteristics, the five-tranche capital structure reflects market-standard sizing ratios, and the OC/IC tests are coded to trip automatically under stress, redirecting cash flows per waterfall priority rules.

The outputs — specifically that the AAA tranche maintains coverage above 130% even at 15% defaults, and equity distributions are extinguished at 12% defaults — were written up in a 2-page investment memo, demonstrating the full analytical cycle from data → model → insight → communication.

07 · Methodology

How the Model Was Built

1

Loan Tape Construction

80‑loan synthetic portfolio with industry codes, spreads (350‑600bps), ratings (B3‑Ba2), and performing/default status. Weighted averages benchmarked against JP Morgan CLO data.

Excel SUMPRODUCTLSTA codes
2

Capital Structure Design

Market‑standard sizing (AAA 61% of capital). Subordination calculated to achieve target ratings. Spreads reflect 2023 new‑issue levels.

3

Waterfall Engine

Nested IF/AND/OR logic replicating priority of payments. OC/IC tests trip automatically, redirecting cash to senior notes on breach. Fully dynamic.

DAX‑style logic in Excel
4

Default & Recovery Scenarios

5%/10%/15% cumulative defaults, 40% recovery (Moody’s senior secured average). Lowest‑rated loans default first to reflect real CLO behaviour.

08 · Key Findings

Six Analytical Outputs From the Model

Finding 01
AAA impervious to defaults up to ~22%
With 39% subordination, AAA maintains >130% OC even at 15% defaults.
Finding 02
Equity distributions extinguished at 10% defaults
OC/IC breaches redirect residual interest, leaving equity zero — binary risk profile confirmed.
Finding 03
BBB offers best risk‑adjusted return
Passes all tests except at 15% default (historically rare), spread of 380bps attractive.
Finding 04
OC test breaches create self‑correcting de‑leveraging
Redirected cash speeds up AAA amortisation, improving AAA OC over time.
Finding 05
Industry concentration materially worsens outcomes
25%+ in a single sector lowers default breakeven by ~40%.
Finding 06
Recovery rate non‑linear impact
30% vs 50% recovery changes BBB from pass to fail at same default rate.
